Eco-Friendly Wedding Coats, Shawls and Cover-Ups

Dec
24
2014

If you are planning a winter wedding, adding a coat or shawl to your ensemble just makes sense. Even winter weddings that take place entirely inside have chilly moments. From romantic pictures with your partner in the snow to just stepping in and out of the car, the crisp air can make your nose turn red as Rudolph’s and your skin get goose bumps.

Shopping for a bridal cover-up is the perfect time to add a touch of green to your big day. You can find amazing layers in natural white and cream fabrics like wool, organic cotton and fair trade silk. Here is some eco-friendly inspiration to get you started: 

1.    Fairy Tale Wedding Capes

For the classic bride with a love of fairy tale romance, nothing beats a hooded cape made from white wool. Wool is a natural, sustainable, eco-friendly material that's extremely warm and durable. Enjoy your cape for years to come or resell it after your big day. 

2. Crocheted Organic Boleros And Shrugs

Organic cotton can be crocheted or knit into warm, soft boleros or long sleeve shrugs. Covering up your arms is key to being warm, and these looks allow the rest of your silhouette (and that gorgeous beadwork) to shine in your photos. Organic cotton is grown without pesticides or fertilizers so it is better for the planet as well as the growers and artisans who work with the fabric.

3. Fair Trade Alpaca Scarves And Shawls

Fair Trade products are ethically produced. Novica works with artists around the world and offers micro financing support (0% interest loans to artisans who want to expand their business and bring more income to their wider communities). Many of these craftspeople work with local sustainable materials, like alpaca wool, which is very warm and comfortable. You can find a wide variety of alpaca wool scarves and shawls on novica.com and know that each purchase is giving back to local communities. 

4. Vintage Wedding Wraps

Anything that has already been made and loved is automatically eco-friendly, as nothing new has to be created. If you want to create a unique, but unified, look for your bridal party, consider getting everyone a vintage shawl online or in thrift shops. They make great gifts and can be enjoyed long after the big day is over. As you can see from the real wedding below, differing shades of whites, creams and soft peaches look amazing when grouped together! 

5. Antique Fur Stoles

Just like with vintage wraps, buying an antique fur is a form of recycling (however, some people believe it is still wrong to wear fur of any kind because it propagates the fashion trend). That said, if you have a fur in the family, or find one at a consignment shop, there is nothing warmer than a fur coat. Faux fur was also popular for decades, and makes a great alternative for vegetarians and vegans. Fur stoles are similar to shawls, but long coats can be very elegant as well. 

These are just a few of the ways to source eco-friendly cover-ups for your wedding. You should also look for hemp, hemp-silk and linen fabric wedding shawls - which are chic and competely natural.
